Importance of Solar Energy

Solar panels have been around since 1883, and have come a long way since then. Solar energy is found across America, used in office buildings, vehicles, and of course, homes. Being almost completely carbon neutral, Solar energy produces less than 60 grams of carbon dioxide per Kilowatt Hour, and after about three years, usually balances out. What truly makes solar power remarkable however, is its ability to be conveniently and cheaply used within any home through solar panels. A solar panel-powered home comes with many benefits besides reducing your footprint. During power outages, solar panels can provide a reliable source of electricity, ensuring essential appliances like refrigerators and medical devices remain functional. Additionally, solar panels can significantly reduce electricity bills over time, offering long-term savings and energy independence.

Basic Information


This method of generating electricity is often used for individual residential projects,
although solar farms are also common for large scale community power. Solar panels work by
capturing sunlight using photovoltaic cells, which convert sunlight into electricity through
the movement of electrons, this energy is then sent off to the various parts of a system. The
best way for prospecting homeowners to get involved with solar is through solar panel
installation companies such as Sunpower, Tesla, and Blue Raven. technologies.

Cost


Initial costs for solar energy usage for homeowners within the United states is
around $2.50 per Kilowatt, or about $11,500 for a six kilowatt system. Important things
to note however is that the average cost of solar has gone down every year since 2009,
and that these prices vary from state to state. However this initial cost is worthwhile
as solar has an extremely high return on investment at around 10%, and within a few
years a six kilowatt solar power system saves the typical household about $1,500 per
year. Electricity companies will also offer bonuses for excess electricity your solar
panels generate. We recommend visiting the Department of Energy To learn more

Tax Rebate Information


There are many government incentive programs in place as of 2024. These can help lower the
cost of initial installment as well as upkeep cost throughout their use. Congress has
passed a Solar Investment Tax Credit for the past several years that now provides a 30%
tax credit for systems installed as of 2024. There are also many local organizations that
can provide homeowners with benefits. You should check out the Department of Energy's solar tax guide
to learn more about federal programs, and check out DSIRE to find out about local and state benefits.
